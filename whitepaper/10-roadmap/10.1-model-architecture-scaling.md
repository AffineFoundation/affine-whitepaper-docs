# 10.1 Model Architecture Scaling

**Scaling rationale.** The current fixed architecture requirement (Qwen3-32B) was chosen to eliminate architectural confounds from evaluation. As the network matures, transitioning to larger-scale base models is a natural next step — increased parameter counts provide more capacity for absorbing environment-specific adaptations through RL fine-tuning while retaining general capabilities.

> The benchmark results in Section 9 suggest that the current 32B architecture can be meaningfully improved through Affine training; larger models would expand the ceiling for what training can achieve.

Such a transition would involve coordinated changes across several areas of the system:

- **Inference layer** — larger models require more GPU memory, potentially necessitating higher-memory instances or a shift toward Affine-operated clusters with dedicated allocation.
- **Anti-copy recalibration** — hidden-state dimensionality changes with model size, so the cosine similarity and norm-deviation thresholds would need re-tuning. Larger models may exhibit different norm-ratio distributions between copies and fine-tunes.
- **Scoring pipeline** — the completeness threshold may need adjustment if increased inference latency reduces the number of tasks completable per scoring round.
- **Training economics** — larger models are more expensive to train but may benefit disproportionately from RL fine-tuning.

The architecture's three-layer separation (Section 4) ensures that a model-size transition is primarily an inference-layer concern — no environment code or scoring logic changes would be needed beyond threshold recalibration.
