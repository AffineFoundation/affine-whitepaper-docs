# 10.6 Conclusion

## System Contributions

This paper has presented Affine, a decentralized system for evaluating and training agentic intelligence through interactive, renewable environments on the Bittensor network. The system makes five contributions:

1. **A plagiarism-resistant scoring mechanism** -- Pareto dominance filtering, ELO-based temporal ratings, and a dual-signal anti-copy detector
2. **Container-orchestration infrastructure (Affinetes)** -- cleanly separates environment execution from model inference
3. **Six evaluation environments** -- five interactive (SWE-Infinite, LiveWeb Arena, MemoryGym, NavWorld, OpenSpiel) and one distributional (DISTILL) -- each targeting a distinct capability axis
4. **Training-friendly design choices** -- deterministic seeding, structured reward signals, and the OpenEnv protocol
5. **Preliminary empirical evidence** -- Affine-trained models outperform the base model on external benchmarks not used in training, including:
   - Stable benchmarks (MCP-Bench, MemoryAgentBench)
   - Code-generation benchmarks (SWE-rebench, HumanEval)
   - Directional snapshot benchmarks (ToolSandbox, Tau2)

## Distinguishing Properties

Several properties distinguish this approach from conventional benchmark design:

- **Renewable task supplies** rather than fixed, resisting saturation and contamination
- **Interactive environments** rather than single-turn, capturing the sequential decision-making that defines agentic behavior
- **First-class infrastructure** -- enabling reproducible evaluation at scale rather than one-off script execution
- **Dual-use design** -- every environment serves as both an evaluation benchmark and an RL training substrate, with deterministic seeding, structured reward signals at varying granularity, and standardized interfaces (OpenEnv) that integrate directly into training loops

## Limitations

### Centralization Trade-Off

While weight-setting occurs on the decentralized Bittensor chain, the scoring pipeline itself -- task generation, execution, and score computation -- runs on centralized backend infrastructure. This design was chosen for operational reasons (scoring requires coordinated access to environments, model endpoints, and the task pool), but it means participants must trust the operator's scoring integrity.

> **Partial mitigation:** Cryptographic signatures on submissions and the public visibility of on-chain weights provide partial auditability, but the scoring backend itself is not independently verifiable by third parties.

### Fixed Model Architecture

Requiring all miners to use Qwen3-32B ensures fair comparison but:

- Limits the diversity of models that can participate
- May not generalize findings to other architectures

### Benchmark Snapshot Limitations

The external benchmark results in Section 6 represent a single cross-sectional comparison, not a longitudinal trajectory:

- The strongest miner (Leary CX) wins 7/7 stable and code benchmarks
- The weakest (Axon1 M19) wins only 2/7
- The snapshot benchmarks (ToolSandbox, Tau2) have incomplete coverage

> **What would strengthen the evidence:** Systematic longitudinal tracking would provide stronger evidence of sustained improvement.

### Environment-Specific Restrictions

- **NavWorld** -- limited to mainland China (71 cities) due to AMap API coverage
- **LiveWeb Arena** -- covers financial and weather domains but not e-commerce or social media
- **MemoryGym** -- empirical composite scores range from 0% to 24.3% across eight models, with most large models clustering between 11-19%
- **OpenSpiel** -- default opponents are random bots, which may not fully stress strategic reasoning

## Central Bet

Affine's central bet is that the right incentive structure, combined with renewable environments and production-grade infrastructure, can turn open participation into sustained intelligence improvement.

The benchmark results in Section 6 provide initial evidence that this bet is paying off:

- The strongest miner wins **7 of 7** stable and code benchmarks against the base model.
- The SWE-rebench results (**12.28 vs. base 0.00**) provide particularly direct evidence that environment-specific training produces transferable capability.

> **The ultimate measure:** Whether this trajectory continues will be measured not by scores on a fixed test set, but by whether the models that emerge from this process are genuinely more capable agents than those trained without it.
