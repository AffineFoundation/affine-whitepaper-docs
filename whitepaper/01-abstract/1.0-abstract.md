# 1. Abstract

*Version: April 2026.*

Affine, Bittensor Subnet 120, is an open evaluation platform focused on RL training, where open-source models by permissionless miner contributors are incentivized to outperform in real-world agentic tasks. The open-source community is constrained by closed organizations' monopoly over the core post-training and RL techniques behind large models. Affine aims to break that monopoly by training open-source models through a fully decentralized approach to match and surpass them. The Affine platform serves as a public mechanism for verifying and rewarding real improvements. Key values of Affine lie in delivering RL environments and reward signals that guide miners through post training. They define capability boundaries and steer the direction of improvement, and turn open competition into a compounding process instead of a single benchmark race. The platform's ecosystem generates robust and multidimensional environments where miners conduct RL post-training against the unified suite. In the next process, a multi-dimensional layer verifies and incentivizes the individual performance of each model using exploit-resistant mechanism. Scoring then runs a winner-takes-all Champion Challenge pipeline with Pareto filtering, which means strictly dominated miners are permanently terminated. The reigning champion receives 100% of emission, losing the crown only after a challenger has established dominance across consecutive checkpoints. Initial empirical results from three independently trained models show improvements over the Original Qwen3-32B base on SWE-ReBench, BrowserComp and memory-related benchmarks. It is foreseeable that affine models will surpass the best open-source models across a wide range of agentic scenarios in the comming months.
