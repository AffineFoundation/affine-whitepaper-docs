# 7.2 Training Philosophy

**No prescribed pipeline.** Affine deliberately provides only evaluation environments and scoring signals -- not a built-in training pipeline. Miners independently choose their:

- RL algorithms
- Hyperparameters
- Training strategies

> **Why this matters:** This design treats training methodology as part of the competitive landscape: the system rewards *outcomes* (model quality across environments) rather than prescribing *process*.

## Methodological Diversity

The deliberate non-prescription of training methods leads to methodological diversity -- different miners may use:

- Different RL frameworks
- Different reward shaping
- Different curriculum strategies

This diversity both increases the probability of discovering effective training approaches and prevents the network from converging on a single, potentially suboptimal, training recipe.

## Geometric Mean Pressure

The geometric mean scoring (Section 6.1, Stage 3) creates strong pressure toward broad competence. Consider the following example:

- A miner who scores **0.95** in six environments but **0.0** in one receives a **near-zero** aggregate -- not a respectable 0.81.

> **Key implication:** This incentivizes broad-spectrum capability improvement rather than narrow specialization in a few environments.

## Training-Friendly Environments

All Affine environments are designed for dual use as both evaluation benchmarks and RL training substrates. They provide:

- **Deterministic seeding** -- for reproducibility
- **Structured reward signals at varying granularity:**
  - Dense per-step shaping in LiveWeb and QQR
  - Per-task outcomes in SWE-Infinite
  - Per-position distributional signals in DISTILL
- **The OpenEnv protocol** (Section 4.4) -- for gym-compatible integration into training loops
