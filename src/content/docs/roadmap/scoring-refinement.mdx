---
title: "10.5 Scoring Mechanism Refinement"
---

Three areas of the scoring system may benefit from refinement as the miner population grows and the environment suite expands.

**Anti-copy integration.** The anti-copy detector currently operates independently from the scoring pipeline, with results stored for review. A natural evolution would be to integrate detection signals more directly into Pareto filtering — potentially automating the exclusion of flagged copies from scoring rounds. This would close the loop between forensic analysis and incentive enforcement.

**Adaptive parameters.** Absence decay parameters and the ELO K-factor schedule were calibrated for the current population size. As the network grows, these may need adaptive tuning — a larger population could require faster convergence or more aggressive decay to prevent rating inflation.

**Curriculum learning.** The geometric mean scoring already incentivizes broad-spectrum capability, but the system does not yet provide tools for structured curriculum learning across environments — for example, scheduling training on environments by difficulty progression, or weighting training samples by environment-specific capability gaps. This would complement the geometric mean incentive by giving miners a more guided path to broad competence.
