---
title: "5.2 Task Renewal"
---

Task renewal is the design choice that most distinguishes Affine environments from fixed benchmarks. It is the property that keeps the evaluation distribution moving faster than any miner can memorize it, and it is what makes every environment simultaneously a valid scoring surface and a valid RL training surface.

### Why Static Datasets Fail a Long-Running Market

A fixed evaluation set has a saturation curve measured in training epochs, not years. Once the set is absorbed into any miner's training data — directly, through scraped contamination, or through a strong teacher model — the scoring signal collapses. Contamination surveys have documented this pattern across standard benchmarks: accuracy numbers stop reflecting capability once memorization enters the loop. A market that settles economic facts on top of scoring cannot run on a substrate with that failure mode.

### Renewable Generation in Practice

Every environment in the current suite keeps its distribution opening up faster than any individual miner consumes it. SWE-Infinite ingests new GitHub PRs continuously; as long as the open-source community keeps committing, the pool is replenished. LiveWeb Arena draws state from the live web (non-repeating by construction) and combines it with templated task specs to produce ≈10^8 distinct configurations. MemoryGym regenerates domains and correction events from seeds; the effective task space is as large as the seed space. NavWorld rotates a weekly salt that re-randomizes SHA256-seeded city graphs, forcing rediscovery of tool-use patterns. OpenSpiel draws seed-indexed trajectories from a space exceeding 10^60. DISTILL's teacher pool grows every time a new top miner appears, so the reference distribution is updated by the market itself.

### What This Gives Training

Because the distribution is inexhaustible, miners have a legitimate RL training substrate — they can run rollouts against the same environment they will be scored on, collect reward signals, and improve. Without renewal, the only safe training option would be to hold the environment out during training, which turns it from a substrate into a test set.

### What This Gives Scoring

For scoring, renewal is the defense against the slow failure mode of every static leaderboard: overfitting to the test distribution. By the time a miner has optimized against any particular snapshot, the distribution has moved on. The scoring pipeline in [§3.2](/mechanism/scoring/) treats same-day scores as comparable because the distribution drifts slowly relative to scoring cadence, but memorization-style strategies see their edge erode round over round. Training substrate for miners and moving test distribution for the scorer — the combination is what closes the loop without either side having to trust the other.
